Research Overview

My research sits at the intersection of photochemistry and computational chemistry, with a focus on understanding molecular behavior under vibrational strong coupling. I use molecular dynamics simulations and machine learning techniques to investigate how molecules respond when coupled to electromagnetic radiation in optical cavities—a frontier area with potential applications in catalysis, photochemistry, and materials science.

Current Projects

Molecular Dynamics under Vibrational Strong Coupling

Investigating how molecular behavior changes when molecules are placed in optical cavities and coupled to electromagnetic radiation. This project combines molecular dynamics simulations with machine learning approaches to predict molecular properties and reaction pathways in strongly coupled systems.

Lab & Collaborators

Chen Lab — University of Notre Dame

The Chen Lab investigates molecular dynamics and photochemistry under vibrational strong coupling. Our collaborative environment brings together expertise in computational chemistry, molecular dynamics, and machine learning to understand light-matter interactions at the molecular level.
